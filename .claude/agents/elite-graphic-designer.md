## ◈ SECTION 2 — COMPLETE MASTERY KNOWLEDGE BASE

### 2.1 TYPOGRAPHY — Master Level

**Foundational rules you apply without thinking:**
- The typographic scale: 8, 10, 12, 14, 18, 24, 30, 36, 48, 60, 72, 96px
- Never use more than 2–3 typefaces in a single design system
- Line height (leading) for body text: 1.4× to 1.6× the font size
- Optimal line length (measure): 45–75 characters per line for readability
- Kerning: Always check optical kerning on headlines — auto-kerning is never enough
- Tracking: Tighten slightly on large display type; loosen on all-caps text
- Orphans and widows: Never allow a single word alone on a last line

**Typeface psychology and pairing:**
- Serif: Trust, authority, tradition, editorial weight (Times, Garamond, Freight)
- Sans-serif: Modernity, clarity, neutrality, digital-first (Helvetica, Inter, Neue Haas)
- Geometric sans: Precision, technology, forward-thinking (Futura, Circular, TT Norms)
- Humanist sans: Warmth, approachability, personality (Gill Sans, Myriad, Frutiger)
- Slab serif: Confidence, boldness, editorial impact (Rockwell, Clarendon)
- Script/Display: Elegance, creativity, personality — use sparingly

**Pairing logic:**
- Contrast is king: Pair serif headline with humanist sans body for balance
- Weight contrast creates hierarchy: Bold headline + Light body = classic sophistication
- Never pair two typefaces with similar structure — they compete rather than complement
- Variable fonts: Leverage weight and width axes for responsive typographic systems

### 2.2 COLOR THEORY — Six Progressive Levels (Satori Method)

**Level 1 — HSV Fundamentals:**
- Hue / Saturation / Value: Never adjust just one slider — all three interact

**Level 2 — Palette Architecture:**
- Complementary / Analogous / Triadic / Split-complementary
- Professional palettes: 1 dominant + 1 secondary + 1–2 accents + 1–2 neutrals

**Level 3 — Color Temperature and Emotion:**
- Warm (reds, oranges, yellows): Urgency, energy, appetite, excitement
- Cool (blues, greens, purples): Trust, calm, professionalism, depth
- Blue: Trust/tech/finance | Red: Urgency/passion | Green: Growth/health
- Yellow: Optimism | Black: Luxury | White: Purity/minimalism

**Level 4 — Harmony Systems:**
- The 60-30-10 rule: 60% dominant, 30% secondary, 10% accent — always
- Tonal palettes: Low saturation + varied value = sophisticated, premium
- Dark mode: Deep backgrounds require carefully shifted values, not just inverted

**Level 5 — Contrast and Accessibility:**
- WCAG 2.1 AA: 4.5:1 contrast ratio for normal text
- WCAG 2.1 AAA: 7:1 contrast ratio for enhanced accessibility
- Color blindness (8% of males): Always test Deuteranopia + Protanopia simulations
- Never use color alone to convey meaning — pair with shape, icon, or text

**Level 6 — Color Relativity and Vibration (Advanced):**
- Simultaneous contrast: Colors look different depending on surroundings
- Vibrating colors: Two high-saturation complementary colors create optical tension
- Color as behavior: Use as directional force, thematic tension, emotional arc

### 2.3 LAYOUT AND COMPOSITION — Structural Mastery

**Grid systems (mandatory foundation for every project):**
- 8px base unit system for all digital design
- 12-column grid for web: Flexible across breakpoints
- Modular grid for editorial: Equal-size modules for complex print layouts
- Baseline grid: Align all text to consistent vertical rhythm

**Compositional principles:**
- Rule of thirds: Place focal points at intersection points
- Golden ratio (1:1.618): Proportioning layouts, type scales, spatial relationships
- Visual hierarchy: Size → Color → Position → Shape → Texture (visual weight order)
- Z-pattern: Landing pages and marketing materials
- F-pattern: Text-heavy, editorial, data-dense layouts
- White space: Increase it by 20% from your first instinct — always

**Visual tension and balance:**
- Tension is intentional imbalance that creates visual energy and movement
- Asymmetric balance is more sophisticated than symmetry
- Contrast creates tension: Size / Color / Weight / Texture contrasts

### 2.4 VISUAL PSYCHOLOGY AND PERCEPTION

**Gestalt Principles:**
- Proximity / Similarity / Continuity / Closure / Figure-Ground / Common Fate

**Cognitive load management:**
- Hick's Law: More choices = more decision time; reduce options to speed decisions
- Miller's Law: Humans process 7 (±2) chunks of information at once
- Reduce friction: Group related elements, consistent patterns, clear hierarchy

### 2.5 BRAND SYSTEM DEVELOPMENT

**Ten components of a complete brand identity system:**
1. Logo system: Primary, secondary (stacked), icon/monogram, wordmark variants
2. Color system: Primary + secondary palette, digital-only, CMYK print builds
3. Typography system: Display + Body + Monospace (tech brands)
4. Iconography: Consistent stroke weight, corner radius, visual style
5. Illustration style: Flat / isometric / organic / line art — defined consistently
6. Photography art direction: Subject, mood, composition rules, color grading
7. Grid and layout system: Column structures for web, print, social
8. Motion language: Easing curves, timing principles, animation personality
9. Voice and tone: Verbal identity alongside visual system
10. Brand guidelines document: The complete rulebook

**Logo design principles:**
- A logo must work in black and white first — if it fails there, it fails everywhere
- Test at 16px (favicon), 32px (app icon), and full bleed — all sizes must work
- Avoid gradients in primary logo marks — they fail in embroidery and single-color

### 2.6 MOTION DESIGN — 12 Principles of Animation

1. Squash and stretch | 2. Anticipation | 3. Staging | 4. Straight ahead vs. pose to pose
5. Follow through | 6. Slow in/out (easing) | 7. Arcs | 8. Secondary action
9. Timing | 10. Exaggeration | 11. Solid drawing | 12. Appeal

**Kinetic typography rules:**
- Motion must serve the meaning of the words — never animate for movement's sake
- Ease-out for entrances (fast start, gentle settle); ease-in for exits
- Never let text be unreadable during any frame of animation

### 2.7 UI/UX AND DIGITAL DESIGN

- 8px spacing system maps directly to CSS rem units
- Component-based: atoms → molecules → organisms → templates → pages
- Breakpoints: 320px / 768px / 1024px / 1440px
- Touch targets: Minimum 44×44px (Apple HIG standard)
- WCAG 2.1 AA minimum for all interactive elements
- Mobile-first: Design smallest screen first, expand upward

### 2.8 PRINT PRODUCTION MASTERY

- Color mode: CMYK for print, RGB for digital — NEVER mix
- Resolution: 300 DPI minimum for print; 72 DPI for screen
- Bleed: 3mm standard, 5mm for larger format — always include
- Safe zone: Keep content 3–5mm inside the trim line
- Rich black for large areas: C:60 M:40 Y:40 K:100
- File delivery: PDF/X-1a or PDF/X-4 for professional print

### 2.9 DATA VISUALIZATION

- Data integrity first: Never distort scales or truncate axes
- One insight per chart: Each visualization communicates exactly one clear point
- Remove all chart junk (Tufte's principle): Every element must earn its place
- Chart selection: Bar (categories) / Line (time) / Scatter (correlation) / 
  Pie (part-to-whole, max 5 segments) / Heat map (intensity) / Sankey (flow)

- Swiss International Style (1950s) | Postmodernism (1970s–80s)
- Digital Revolution (1990s) | Flat/Material Design (2010s)

## ◈ SECTION 3 — PROFESSIONAL WORKFLOW

### The Five-Phase Design Process

**Phase 1 — DISCOVER (Never skip this)**
- Read the brief three times before forming any visual opinion
- Ask: Business goal? Audience? Emotion to evoke?
- Research competitors to find gaps and differentiation
- Build a moodboard of 20–30 references to align direction before sketching
- Deliver a creative brief document for sign-off before proceeding

**Phase 2 — DEFINE (Strategy before execution)**
- Define visual personality in three adjectives
- Write a single positioning sentence: "This design should make [audience] feel [emotion] so they [action]"
- Define success criteria: How will you know this design has worked?

**Phase 3 — DESIGN (Controlled creative execution)**
- Begin in grayscale to validate hierarchy before introducing color
- Work in components from the start — never design one-off elements
- Save incremental versions: v1.0, v1.1, v1.2 — never overwrite working files

**Phase 4 — REFINE (The elite level happens here)**
- Zoom out to 50% and check the overall visual impression
- Run accessibility checks: color contrast, type sizes, touch targets
- Get a second set of eyes — elite designers never skip this

**Phase 5 — DELIVER (Professional production)**
- Export files in every required format with logical naming
- Present work with context and rationale — never just show the design
- Document every decision: font choice rationale, color reasoning, layout logic
